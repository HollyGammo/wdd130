Snake Bits

In case of a snake bite, look at the marks left by the snake. A venomous snake will leave two very pronounced fang marks at the top of their bite and there may not be any other tooth marks. A non-venomous snake will have small marls left behind by their teeth but there will not be any large fang marks. If you do receive a venomous snake bite, follow the guidlines below.

What TO DO For a Snake Bite

• Stay calm.
• Call 911 immediately to be evaluated.
• Take off anything constricting the area, such as a ring or watch.
• Elevate the bite. Position the affected area at or above heart level.
• Make note of the time the bite occured, outline the swollen or red area with a marker. This will aid the doctors in treating the bite.
• Go to the emergency room as soon as possible.

What NOT TO DO For a Snake Bite

• DON'T apply a tourniquet or a constriction band.
• DON'T apply ice, it can cause tissue damage.
• DON'T apply heat.
• DON'T cut the affected area and/or attempt to suck the venom out of the wound. This is ineffective and can cause infection.
• DON'T apply any type of lotions or ointments.
• DON'T Try to capture the snake.

Signs and Symptoms of a Snake Bite

Signs or symptoms of a snake bite may vary depending on the type of snake, but may include:

Puncture marks at the wound • Redness, swelling, bruising, bleeding, or blistering around the bite.
• Severe pain and tenderness at the site of the bite.
• Nausea, vomiting, or diarrhea.
• Labored breathing (in extreme cases, breathing may stop altogether).
• Rapid heart rate, weak pulse, low blood pressure.
• Disturbed vision.
• Metallic, mint, or rubber taste in the mouth.
• Increased salivation and sweating.
• Numbness or tingling around face and/or limbs.
• Muscle twitching.
